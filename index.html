BIO

Andrei Pancu is a contemporary artist residing and working in Ottawa, Canada. Born in 1968 in Bucharest, Romania, Pancu is known for inducing patinas on ferrous metals and impressing regular patterns to create layered compositions of abstract, complex forms. His ambiguous motifs allude at times to the natural, others times to the man-made. The theme, though not explicitly set, is one of events suspended in time, the tone subdued, serene, contemplative. Heavily influenced by the natural properties of ferrous metals, the color palette is predominantly earthy, featuring deep browns, ochres, and muted reds. Pancu crafts his own frames out of mild steel to support free-standing as well as wall-mounted works.

The interplay of entropy and order are both the subject and the process of Andrei Pancu's work. Order, in the form of conscious, willful intent is present in the regular patterns Pancu uses to temper the flow of the ever-evolving, chaotic patina. The struggle to impose, let alone maintain, order against a relentless force that aims to do the opposite parallels the human condition. Through his work, Pancu aims to invite interpretive engagement over narrative clarity. He employs incomplete, ambiguous signifiers in his patterns to entice the subconscious into filling in missing references. In 2024, Pancu launched his seminal series, "Entropy and Order".

Andrei Pancu studied Fine Arts at the Université de Montréal.
