BIO

Andrei Pancu is a contemporary artist residing and working in Ottawa, Canada. Born in 1968 in Bucharest, Romania, Pancu is known for inducing patinas on ferrous metals and impressing regular patterns to create layered compositions of abstract, complex forms. His ambiguous motifs allude at times to the natural, others times to the man-made. The tone is subdued, serene, contemplative. The theme is one of events suspended in time. Heavily influenced by the natural properties of ferrous metals, the color palette is predominantly earthy, featuring deep browns, ochres, and muted reds. Pancu crafts his own frames out of mild steel to create free-standing as well as wall-mounted works.

The interplay of entropy and order is both the subject and the process of Andrei Pancu's work. Order, in the form of conscious, willful intent is applied through stamps and masks that serve to temper the flow of the ever-evolving, chaotic patina. The struggle to impose, let alone maintain, order against a relentless force intent on doing just the opposite is akin to the human condition. In his seminal series, "Entropy and Order", launched in 2024, Pancu pits these opposing forces against each other, putting them into dialectical play. Through his work, Pancu promotes interpretive engagement over narrative clarity through patterns of incomplete, ambiguous signifiers that aim to entice the subconscious into filling in the missing references with scenes and events from personal experience.

Andrei Pancu studied Fine Arts at the Université de Montréal.
